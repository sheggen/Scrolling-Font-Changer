When a portrait is hung on a wall, it implies that the subject possesses a level of ownership over the immediate space (or, perhaps, the entire building). As a result, it can be impossible to enter a room without encountering the figure and the legacy they represent. This proposal endows viewers with agency to determine how they coexist with a group of portraits and the space they occupy.

Each of these four portrait is attached to a movable section of wall, which places control in the hands of viewers. For example, a visitor could choose to turn a wall section around, effectively hiding an image from the gallery. Alternately, the walls can be pushed into different configurations, offering visitors a chance to experiment with the arrangement of space.

We invite you to use the metal bars to push and pull these panels to create your own viewing experience. If you would like some help moving the sections, we are happy to assist. Just call the Art Office at 859-985-3658 and someone will come to help.

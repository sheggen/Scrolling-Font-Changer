When a portrait is hung on a wall, it often implies a level of ownership over the immediate space and, sometimes, the entire building. It becomes impossible to enter the room and not encounter the person and the legacy they represent. This proposal endows viewers with agency to determine how they coexist with the portraits. By moving the sections of the wall graced by the portraits, some control is in the hands of the visitors. Turning the wall section around effectively removes the painting from the gallery. Alternatively, the four walls excerpts can be moved into the center of the gallery to create a smaller space in which to view the portraits.

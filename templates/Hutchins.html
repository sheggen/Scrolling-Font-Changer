Period Rooms are installations within museums that aim to capture the material essence of an era, providing the viewer with a representation of what it might have looked like to inhabit a space during a particular time period. However, recent discussions have made apparent that period rooms are more a museum construction than historical representation, typically incorporating more objects than would actually exist within a living space, meshing together materials that might be from the same era, but would be unlikely to cohabit a room, among other discrepancies. Though both are presented in contexts that suggest historical accuracy, here we consider the implications of period rooms and portraits as institutional fabrications rather than fact.
